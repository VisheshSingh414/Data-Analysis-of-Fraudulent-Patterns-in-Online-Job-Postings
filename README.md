# 🛡️ Fraud Detection in Online Job Postings

## 📌 Project Overview

The rise of online recruitment platforms has made job searching easier than ever. However, it has also created opportunities for fraudulent employers to post fake job listings and scam job seekers.

This project analyzes online job postings to identify patterns associated with fraudulent advertisements. Using data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and interactive Power BI dashboards, the project uncovers the characteristics that distinguish legitimate job postings from fraudulent ones.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Fake job postings can:

- Mislead job seekers
- Steal personal information
- Cause financial losses
- Damage trust in recruitment platforms

The objective of this project is to identify common fraud indicators and provide insights that can help recruiters and job platforms detect suspicious listings early.

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 1. Missing Company Information is a Strong Fraud Indicator

Many fraudulent job postings lack detailed company profiles, making transparency a key factor in identifying suspicious listings.

### 2. Certain Industries Have Higher Fraud Rates

Fraudulent postings are concentrated in specific industries, indicating targeted scam activities.

### 3. Remote Opportunities Require Additional Verification

Telecommuting and work-from-home opportunities show a higher proportion of fraudulent listings.

### 4. Incomplete Job Descriptions Increase Risk

Listings with vague descriptions and missing requirements are more likely to be fraudulent.

### 5. Geographic Fraud Patterns Exist

Certain regions exhibit higher concentrations of suspicious job postings.
